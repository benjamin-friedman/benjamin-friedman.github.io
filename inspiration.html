Hans zimmer is the greatest composer of the modern era and one of the greatest composers of all time. The breadth of his work is unmatched having composed some of the most beautiful, powerful, and emotional music ever written in the soundtracks of countless major motion pictures. Some of his best work comes from The Dark Knight trilogy, The Pirates Of The Caribbean series, Inception, and Interstellar amongst many other films. From The Dark Knight trilogy his best compositions include Corynorhinus, Myotis, Molossus, A Dark Knight, Agent Of Chaos, and Why Do We Fall. One of the most beautiful scores ever composed is Marry Me from Pirates Of The Caribbean: At World's End in which it was an unreleased track with elements of it being in other tracks. Other incredible songs he has composed include Time from Inception, First Steps, No Time For Caution, and Mountains from Interstellar, and Final Ascent from No Time To Die. A cellist named Stjepan Hauser did an incredible cello rendition of his composition Now We Are Free from Gladiator. Many of the cello tracks here are from Hauser who is known for both his interpretations of traditional classical cello songs and renditions of songs not originally made for the cello. It should be noted that Hans collaborated with James Newton Howard on the first two films in The Dark Knight Trilogy, Klaus Badelt in the first Pirates of the Caribbean film, and Klaus Badelt once again along with Lisa Gerrard in Gladiator so the credit goes to them as well.

Frederic Chopin is the greatest classical piano composer of all time. Other composers have produced piano pieces undeniably as good as Chopin's and that statement does not take anything away from them at all. To diminish Franz Liszt's Liebestraum No. 3, Ludwig van Beethoven's forever immortal Moonlight Sonata 1st movement, or the works of numerous other composers would be an unfathomable and untenable crime at best. But if one is to go down the route of choosing one single classical piano composer to rule them all then it is Chopin because there is no composer who can match the quantity of remarkable compositions that Chopin produced. Chopin did not just write one or two remarkable compositions but rather did so time and time again to the point where it is pondered how he even began to achieve this thus rendering the question which always inevitably comes to mind when dealing with geniuses. His series of 21 nocturnes are one of the most beautiful musical compositions ever written. Though every nocturne is a standalone work of art some of the best include Nocturne In B Flat Minor Op. 9 No. 1, Nocturne In E Minor Op. 72 No. 1, Nocturne in C Sharp Minor Op. Posth., and Nocturne In C Minor Op. Posth. which are his 1st, 19th, 20th, and 21st nocturnes respectively. Nocturnes 20 and 21 were not appreciated until after his death as they were published posthumously. This is especially regretful for his 20th nocturne as it is arguably his most famous and undoubtedly one of the most beautiful he ever composed. In addition to his nocturnes his work includes 27 preludes (one of them unfinished), 27 etudes, 3 piano sonatas, and 4 ballades. Rivaling the beauty of his nocturnes are his 4th and 15th preludes known as Prelude In E Minor Op. 28 No. 4 and Prelude In D Flat Major Op. 28 No. 15 more commonly known as Raindrop Prelude. The beauty, emotion, power, and technical prowess of his first and most famous Ballade, Ballade No. 1 In G Minor Op. 23, led to it being known as "La Ballade" or "The Ballade". Chopin died fairly young at the age of 39, and somewhere out there in the universe are all of the compositions he would have written yet to be heard or experienced.

Franz Schubert is most well known for Serenade but has other incredible compositions as well such as Impromptu In G Flat Major Op. 90 No. 3 and Piano Trio No. 2 In E Flat Major Movement II Op. 100. The cello part of Piano Trio No. 2 In E Flat Major Movement II is one of the most emotional cello scores ever written only enhanced by its pairing with the piano forming a kind of musical duality as is always the case when the piano and cello unite. Schubert died even younger than Chopin at the age of 31.

Like Chopin, Liszt is known for his beautiful, elegant, and technically demanding piano pieces such as the Liebestraum No. 3, Consolation No. 3, Romance O Pourquoi Donc In E Minor, and his piano arrangement of Paganini's violin composition La Campanella. The unique beauty and emotion of Liebestraum No. 3 is unmatched by any other piano song, and the beauty and emotion of Consolation No. 3 and Romance O Pourqoi Donc parallels that of Chopin's work. He also did a piano arrangement of Schubert's Serenade which was originally written for strings, and he was able to capture the same emotion and beauty of that song on the piano as Schubert was on the strings.

Beethoven is most well known for his symphonies and Moonlight Sonata 1st Movement. The emotion and depth of Moonlight Sonata 1st Movement cannot be underscored enough, and it is a timeless piece that genuinely exists in its own realm. Fewer know of Moonlight Sonata 3rd Movement which is regarded as one of the most technically challenging piano pieces ever. Like Chopin's "La Ballade", Moonlight Sonata 3rd Movement revealed unseen heights, dimensions, and possibilities within the piano that were hidden from the world prior to its composition. The fact that Beethoven was able to compose some of his music despite becoming deaf is a testament to how much of a musical genius he sincerely was.

Some of Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart's best work includes Fantasia In D Minor, Serenade For Winds 3rd Movement, and The Requiem In D Minor. While writing The Requiem, Mozart's health was actively deteriorating and he ultimately died before its completion. Despite the controversy and ambiguity surrounding The Requiem's completion that arises from this, it is true that Mozart was dying during the process of its composition, and in that sense he was writing The Requiem for himself making the piece all the more compelling.

Some of Johann Sebastian Bach's best work includes Toccata and Fugue In D Minor, Air On The G String, and his famous Cello Prelude In G Major. The heavy beauty and almost divine transcendence of Toccata and Fugue In D Minor separates it from every other piece ever written for the organ. Tomaso Albinoni is most well known for his piece Adagio In G Minor also known as "Albinoni's Adagio". Camille Saint-Saens is most well known for his piece The Swan. Albinoni's Adagio and The Swan are two of the most beautiful compositions ever written. The Swan is traditionally played by a cello accompanied by either a harp or a piano. Neither version is superior but rather they are just different. Thus, The Swan becomes even further remarkable because it is a single song out of which are birthed two different experiences each having their own unique beauty.

Erik Satie composed a series of Gnossiennes and Gymnopedies with the first composition in each series being the best and most well known. There is a deep level of calmness within his first Gymnopedie, and his first Gnossienne is a hauntingly beautiful piece for which it is difficult to pinpoint which emotion it strikes at. The essence of these compositions truly situates them uniquely amongst all other pieces of music ever composed.

Most would probably recognize Claude Debussy's Claire De Lune but fewer have probably heard Reverie or Arabesque No. 1. Claire De Lune and Reverie both capture a kind of soothing and tranquil beauty. The true magnificence of Clair De Lune lies within the fact that Debussy is able to maintain this even in the parts where the velocity increases significantly from its slow-tempoed beginning. That's not at all to say that Clair De Lune is therefore inherently superior to Reverie as they are both equally beautiful. Rather, it is a feat worth mentioning because it is perhaps easier to achieve that maintenance in a song like Reverie where the tempo remains slow throughout.

Rachmaninoff and Shostakovich are both Soviet-era Russian composers. It is difficult to find a piece of music more powerful than the opening of the first movement of Rachmaninoff's Piano Concerto No. 2.

Harry Gregson-Williams's Narnia Lullaby although brief in duration is one of the most strikingly beautiful and transcendent pieces of music ever composed and is often played on the duduk. Steve Jablsonky and James Newton Howard are known for their contributions to motion picture soundtracks like Hans Zimmer. Steve Jablonsky is best known for his work in the Transformers series, and James Newton Howard is known for his work in many films including The Hunger Games series and his collaboration with Hans Zimmer in the first two films of The Dark Knight trilogy. Nicholas Hooper, Patrick Doyle, and Alexandre Desplat all contributed to the Harry Potter series soundtrack. Howard Shore composed The Lord Of The Rings series soundtrack. Michael Giacchino's Married Life from Up is a beautiful song, and Bear McCreary did the music for The Walking Dead TV series.

Steve Vai is one of the greatest guitarists of all time and there are no tracks that can compare to For The Love Of God or Tender Surrender. The greatest metal band of all time is Slipknot. However, the greatest metal song of all time is Bleed by Meshuggah. The drums on that song are so difficult that the calm interlude in the middle was written just so that the drummer Tomas Haake could have a break and rest. The part that starts right after that is one of the greatest pieces of music ever written.

The two poems are Dylan Thomas's Do Not Go Gentle Into That Good Night and William Ernest Henley's Invictus. Do Not Go Gentle Into That Good Night gained additional popularity in the modern era due to its use in the movie Interstellar, and Invictus was Nelson Mandela's favorite poem. He cited its words as something that would give him strength during all of the years he was wrongfully imprisoned. The last recording is Gary Oldman's reading of the ending of Charles Dickens's A Tale Of Two Cities.
