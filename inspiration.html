Hans zimmer is the greatest composer of the modern era and one of the greatest composers of all time. His breadth of compositions within countless motion pictures is unmatched by anyone else. Some of his best work comes from The Dark Knight trilogy, The Pirates Of The Carribean series, Inception, and Interstellar amongst others. From The Dark Knight trilogy some of his best compositions are Why Do We Fall, Agent Of Chaos, Molossus, Corynorhinus, Myotis, and A Dark Knight. He collaborated with James Newton Howard on some of the songs from the trilogy. One of the most beautiful scores he ever composed was Marry Me from Pirates Of The Carribean: At World's End in which it was actually an unreleased track with elements of it being in other tracks. Other incredible songs he's composed include Time from Inception, First Steps, No Time For Caution, and Mountains from Interstellar, and Final Ascent from No Time To Die. He collaborated with Klaus Badelt and Lisa Gerrard for some of his work in Gladiator, and a cellist named Stjepan Hauser did an incredible cello rendition of Now We Are Free. Many of the cello tracks here are from Hauser who is known for both his interpretations of traditional classical cello songs and also doing renditions of songs not originally made for the cello.

Frederic Chopin is the greatest classical piano composer of all time. Other composers such as Liszt have produced solo piano pieces as good as Chopin's but there is no composer who can match the quantity of incredible solo piano music that Chopin has produced. His series of 21 nocturnes are one of the most beautiful musical compositions ever written with some of the best being Nocturne In E Minor Op. 72 No. 1, Nocturne in C Sharp Minor Op. Posth., Nocturne In C Minor Op. Posth, and Nocturne In B Flat Minor Op. 9 No. 1 which are his 19th, 20th, 21st, and 1st nocturnes respectively. Nocturnes 20 and 21 were not published until after his death which is why they are marked as posthumous. The nocturne recordings here are by Francois Chaplain, and other pianists have great interpretations like Nelson Goerner who is the pianist in the second interpretation of Nocturne In C Sharp Minor Op. Posth. Rivaling the beauty of his nocturnes are his 4th and 15th preludes known as Prelude In E Minor Op. 28 No. 4 and Prelude In D Flat Major Op. 28 No. 15 also known as Raindrop Prelude. The recording of Raindrop Prelude is by Ivo Pogorelich, and Daniil Trifonov and Pavel Kolesnikov also have great interpretations. In addition to his nocturnes and preludes he did a series of etudes, piano sonatas, and four ballades. The beauty, emotion, power, and technical prowess of his first and most famous Ballade, Ballade No. 1 In G Minor Op. 23, led to it being known as "La Ballade" or "The Ballade". The recording is by Seong-Jin Cho whose interpretation of that piece is one of the best ever. Chopin died fairly young at the age of 39. Somewhere out there in the universe are all of the compositions he would've written yet to be heard or experienced.

Franz Schubert is most well known for Serenade but has other incredible compositions as well such as Impromptu In G Flat Major Op. 90 No. 3 and Piano Trio No. 2 In E Flat Major Movement II Op. 100. The recording of Impromptu In G Flat Major is by Olga Jegunova and is the most powerful of all interpretations, and Khatia Buniatishvili also has a great and slightly gentler interpretation. Schubert died at an even younger age than Chopin at the age of 31. Serenade was originally composed for strings but Franz Liszt did a piano only arrangement of it. Like Chopin, Liszt is known for his beautiful, elegant, and technically challenging piano pieces such as Liebestraum No. 3, Consolation No. 3, Romance O Pourquoi Donc In E Minor, and La Campanella which is a piano arrangement of Paganini's La Campanella originally made for the violin. The unique beauty of Liebestraum No. 3 is unmatched by any other piano song and the recording is by Khatia Buniatishvili who has the best interpretation of that piece.

Ludwig van Beethoven is most well known for his symphonies and his forever immortal Moonlight Sonata 1st Movement, but fewer know of Moonlight Sonata 3rd Movement which is regarded as one of the most technically challenging piano pieces. The origins of the recording of the 1st movement are unknown but Mitsuko Uchida has one of the best interpretations of it. The recording of the 3rd movement is from a pianist named Rousseau, and Daniel Barenboim also has a great interpretation which is one of the most unique of them all. The fact that Beethoven was able to compose some of his music while becoming progressively deaf is a testament to how much of a musical genius he truly was. Some of Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart's best work includes Fantasia In D Minor, Serenade For Winds, and The Requiem which he famously wrote while dying and ultimately died before he could complete it. Some of Johann Sebstian Bach's best work includes Toccata and Fugue In D Minor, Air On The G String, and his famous Cello Prelude In G Major. Toccata and Fugue In D Minor is arguably one of the greatest pieces ever composed for the organ. Tomaso Albinoni is most well known for his piece Adagio known as "Albinoni's Adagio" and the recording is by Hauser. Camille Saint-Saens is most well known for his piece The Swan. The recording of The Swan with the harp and cello is by Hauser and the recording with the piano and cello is by Kathryn Stott and Yo-Yo Ma.

Erik Satie composed a series of six Gnossiennes and three Gymnopedies with the first being the best of both of them. His first Gnossienne is truly unlike any other song ever created. The origins of the first interpretation of Gnossienne 1 are unknown but the second interpretation is by Alice Sara Ott. Most have probably recognize Claude Debussy's Claire De Lune but fewer would probably have heard Reverie or Arabesque No. 1. The recording of Reverie is also by Alice Sara Ott. Rachmaninoff and Shostakovich are both Soviet-era Russian composers, and the first movement of Rachmaninoff's Piano Concerto No. 2 really captures the Russian spirit. The recording is by Anna Fedorova.

Harry Gregson-Williams's Narnia Lullaby is one of the most remarkable pieces of short music ever composed and is played on the duduk. Steve Jablsonky and James Newton Howard are known for their contributions to motion picture soundtracks like Hans Zimmer. Steve Jablonsky is best known for his work from the Transformers series and James Newton Howard from many movies including The Hunger Games series and his collaboration with Hans Zimmer in The Dark Knight Trilogy in which he contributed to the first two films Batman Begins and The Dark Knight. Nicholas Hooper, Patrick Doyle, and Alexandre Desplat all contributed significantly to the Harry Potter series soundtrack. Howard Shore composed The Lord Of The Rings soundtrack. Michael Giacchino's Married Life from Up is a beautiful song, and Bear McCreary did the music for The Walking Dead TV series.

Steve Vai is one of the greatest guitarists of all time and there are no tracks that can match For The Love Of God or Tender Surrender. The greatest metal band of all time is Slipknot due to the quantity of incredible tracks they've produced from Skin Ticket to Disasterpiece to All Out Life and even non-metal tracks like Snuff. However, the greatest metal song of all time is Bleed by Meshuggah. The drums on that song are so difficult that the calm interlude in the middle was put in there just so that the drummer Tomas Haake could rest and have a break. The part that starts right after that is one of the greatest pieces of music ever written.

The two poems are Dylan Thomas's Do Not Go Gentle Into That Good Night and William Ernest Henley's Invictus. Do Not Go Gentle Into That Good Night gained additional popularity in the modern era due to its use in the movie Interstellar, and Invictus was Nelson Mandela's favorite poem. He cited its words as something that would give him strength during all of the years he was wrongfully imprisoned. The last recording is Gary Oldman's reading of the ending of Charles Dickens' A Tale Of Two Cities.
